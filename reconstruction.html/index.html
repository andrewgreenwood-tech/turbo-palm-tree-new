  • Reconstruction
    • Key Decisions Post-Civil War
      • Fate of seceded states
      • Rights of freed slaves
    • Opposing Plans
      • Lincoln's Plan (1863)
        • General amnesty for loyalty oath
        • Accepted Union's slavery requirements
        • Excluded Confederate officials/military officers
        • Malice toward none, charity for all (1865)
      • Radical Republicans' Plan
        • Prevent Confederate leaders' return to power
        • Federal guarantee of African American voting rights
      • Johnson's Plan (1865)
        • Resembled Lincoln's
        • Amnesty for all except officials/wealthy planters
        • Congressional outrage over former Confederates elected
        • Anger over Black Codes
    • Key Amendments
      • Fourteenth Amendment (1866/1868)
        • Blocked Black Codes
        • Granted citizenship (born/naturalized)
        • Due process of law
        • Equal protection of the laws
        • Military Reconstruction Act (1867)
        • Turning point in US history
        • Expanded federal power over states
        • Extended civil rights
        • Doctrine of incorporation (Bill of Rights to states)
        • Empowered Civil Rights Movement (1950s-60s)
      • Fifteenth Amendment
        • Increased tension between Radical Republicans and Johnson
        • Johnson's impeachment (Senate acquitted)
        • Grant elected President (1868)
